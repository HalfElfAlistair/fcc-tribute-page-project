Tommy Wiseau: The Enigma

As well as writing, directing, producing and financing The Room, Tommy Wiseau also starred as the lead character 'Johnny'.

Introduction

Little is truly known about Tommy Wiseau, despite a lot of research from fans and even a book partly detailing his life.

A clear picture of his origin is yet to emerge, with various suggestions offered by inquisitive fans.

The main thing we do know is that he is responsible for "The Room"; a film many claim to be the worst of all time. Released in 2003 Wiseau's masterpiece has gained a cult following and even a film partly about it's making.

Where is Tommy from?

Although Wiseau considers himself an American these days, he was not born there.

His country of origin has long been a subject of speculation for The Rooms cult following.

Europe

The Disaster Artist, written by co-star Greg Sestero, seems to shed light on Tommy's past.

The author describes an upbringing in Eastern Europe before a move to France. From there it is claimed Wiseau found his way over to Louisiana in the USA.

Due to how close Sestero is to Wiseau, this account is the most plausible. Greg does acknowledge how difficult it is to obtain information on his friend's past though.

Additionally, pinpointing an exact city or even country for TW's beginnings has proven difficult.

This leaves room for debate with some rather wild rumours...

The Past

Let's be honest, it's the wild theories that are the most interesting. Wiseau certainly doesn't disappoint when it comes to those.

His somewhat peculiar accent, coupled with his vampiric looks, could lead people to believe he's actually from hundreds of years ago.

Adding to his 'European' origins, we could even vouch for him as a Transylvanian native like Dracula. Plus he has claimed to have grown up in New Orleans, a place also considered a hotspot by vampire theorists.

Wiseau has supposedly written a film about vampires and is even known to have joked about being one.

There are scenes in The Room where he sets foot outside during daylight hours, so this rumour may be easily debunked.

Space

Some fans have perhaps over analysed the script in The Room but it has lead to an amusing theory about Tommy

During a fight at the party near the films end, Mark (played by Greg) exclaims "what planet are you on?". Could it be that Wiseau inserted this line as a subtle hint towards an extra terrestrial origin?

Of course this is a fantastical idea to be taken with a pinch of salt. It did gain some ground with Marvel though, they made reference to Wiseau being a alien crime lord in a Captain Marvel comic.

The Bottomless Bank Account

Even before the Disaster Artist was published, a big question fans of The Room had was 'where did Wiseau find $6,000,000 for the movie?'

And the release of the book only fueled the fire in this regard.

On cashing a cheque, a bank employee describes Wiseaus account as 'a bottomless pit'.

Let's take a look at some of the likely and unlikely possibilities.

An Entrepeneur

Sestero does provide a story of how Tommy Wiseau acquired his wealth, from conversations with the man himself.

As usual it lacks a lot of detail that would help paint a clear picture.

Taken at face value Wiseau took various jobs and developed a knack for sales before moving into retail business and then property.

This is backed up to a decent extent as footage of his 'Steet Fashions USA' commercial is widely available.

Additionally the coffee shop scene in The Room was filmed on one of his retail properties.

Why Wiseau used a parking lot and greenscreen to film rooftop scenes, when he had a perfectly good rooftop available at these properties, is a source of debate in The Disaster Artist.

Money Laundering

There are claims that The Room is actually a big, huge money laundering scheme and Wiseau could be part of or even king of a criminal empire.

This coincidentally links to the alien theory mentioned earlier and it is likely Marvel took some inspiration from the theory when drafting their comic script.

The secrecy surrounding Wiseau does lend itself to someone with a nefarious background.

As of yet there haven't been any run-ins with law enforecemnt, other than a Canadian court dispute about a documentary on The Room. Thus it is likely his money and film are both legitimate.

Additionally, Sestero considers the ciminal theories to be dubious and he's the best source we have on Tommy's background.

DB Cooper

Like the vampire theory for Wiseau's origins, there is a wonderfully entertaining story that circulated about TW's wealth.

In 1971 DB Cooper (an alias) hijacked a plane, acquired $200,000 ransom and parachuted out, never to be seen again.

A 2014 xkcd web comic propsed a comparison of Cooper and Wiseau, using a few vague points.

Of course this was almost certainly intended as a joke but people have run away with the idea.

It is fairly easily debunked. For example, even if we assume he's older than he claims, Wiseau would still have been fairly young back in 1971.

Tommy has also subsequently denied the link when asked about it.
